The Threat

Why QML Safety Is a Neglected Frontier

The AI safety community has made meaningful progress on alignment, interpretability, and containment. Almost none of that work accounts for what happens when quantum computing reaches meaningful scale.

Quantum Machine Learning is not an imminent crisis, but the 5–15 year horizon to credible quantum advantage in relevant domains is shorter than the typical research-to-deployment cycle for safety infrastructure. We are in the preparation window, and it is largely unoccupied.

Crucially, quantum advantage will not arrive evenly. Nation-states and well-capitalized actors will acquire it first. Without proactive defense research, QML capabilities become a unilateral advantage over existing safety mechanisms, held by precisely those with the fewest constraints on how they are used.

On timelines: We are not claiming quantum computers will imminently break AI safety. We claim the research cycle to build robust defenses is long enough that starting in five years may already be too late. The asymmetry between offense and defense timelines is the core risk.

Alignment Mechanism Attacks

QML optimization techniques may identify and exploit weaknesses in models that classical methods cannot find at scale, effectively breaking trained safety behaviors.

Unverifiable Deceptive Systems

Quantum-assisted training could produce models whose internal representations are computationally intractable to inspect with classical interpretability tools, creating systems that cannot be verified as non-deceptive.

Containment Protocol Failure

Existing containment and shutdown protocols assume classical computational bounds. Quantum-enhanced agents may exploit timing or procedural vulnerabilities in these protocols that classical agents cannot.

Access Concentration

If safety defenses require quantum resources to implement, organizations without quantum access become permanently disadvantaged, concentrating safe AI among only few.

Our Plan

Four Workstreams. One Mandate: Defense First.

Each workstream produces concrete, openly published outputs, threat models, tools, architecture specifications, and practitioner guides, accessible to all organizations regardless of whether they hold quantum resources.

01

Threat Evaluation

Systematically evaluate how QML attack vectors could compromise current alignment and containment protocols. Output: a peer-reviewed threat taxonomy and severity framework.

02

Verification Frameworks

Develop formal and empirical frameworks for verifying that QML-trained systems cannot pursue hidden goals that classical interpretability tools would miss. Output: open-source verification tools and methodology.

03

Quantum-Resistant Architectures

Design safety architectures robust under quantum-assisted adversarial pressure and implementable without quantum resources. Output: implementable architecture specifications.

04

Open Publication

All findings published openly with explicit accessibility for under-resourced organizations. Capacity-building workshops for AI safety teams without quantum access. Output: public reports, workshop series, and practitioner guides.
