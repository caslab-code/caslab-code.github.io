CASLAB Day 2019 will be held on May 3rd (Friday) from 1:30pm to 5:30pm in the Mann Engineering Student Center (Room 107) in Dunham Lab, 10 Hillhouse Ave.

The event is open to the public.

Coffee and light snacks will be served.

Student Presenations -- 1:30pm to 3:00pm

Short (15 min.) talks will be presented by CASLAB graduate and undergraduate students describing their current research on architecture and hardware security.

1:30pm to 1:45pm: Adam Wolnikowski, Parthib Samadder, and Samantha Bleykhman -- Cloud FPGA Accelerator AXI Bridge and Synchronous and Asynchronous FIFOs

1:45pm to 2:00pm: Ilias Giechaskiel -- Measuring Long Wire Leakage with Ring Oscillators in Cloud FPGAs

2:00pm to 2:15pm: Shanquan Tian -- Cloud FPGA Security

2:15pm to 2:30pm: Wen Wang -- Post-Quantum Crypto Accelerators: XMSS

2:30pm to 2:45pm: Shuwen Deng -- Secure TLBs

2:45pm to 3:00pm: Wenjie Xiong -- Dynamic PUFs and Software Protection

Break and Poster Session -- 3:00pm to 4:00pm

Posters of the graduate and undergraduate students' work will be presented during the break.

4:00pm to 4:45pm: Security Tutorial Part 1: Secure Processor Caches

Shuwen Deng will present a tutorial on secure caches.

4:45pm to 5:30pm: Security Tutorial Part 2: Transient Execution Attacks and Mitigations

Wenjie Xiong will present a tutorial on Spectre and Meltdown.

Wrap Up -- 5:30pm
